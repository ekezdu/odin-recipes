CURRY RECIPE

Description

My personal take on japanese curry, using the typical golden curry packet, rice, potatoes, and carrots. I generally cook it in bulk, and freeze the rest in portions, and save a bit to eat for lunch or dinner the next day. My whole family loves it, but be sure to keep in mind the spice level when cooking for others. I have found that in the US, for some reason, mild and hot are much milder than overseas.

EQUIPMENT

  • Large pot, preferably a stew pot
  • Stovetop
  • Knives

INGREDIENTS

  • Golden Curry packet of your desired level
  • 3 medium-large potatoes quartered
  • 3 medium-large carrots cut into ½ inch coins
  • Medium yellow onion, minced
  • Maggi seasoning
  • 1-2 lbs of beef, you can use ground beef, but I prefer chuck beef, cut into 1 inch squares
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • ½ cup Soy Sauce
  • (Optional) 3 cremini mushrooms minced
  • 4 cups water
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • If not using mushrooms, I recommend umami seasoning
  • (Optional)1 tablespoon mirin,red wine or rice vinegar
  • Cornstarch, as needed

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. If using beef chuck, sear beef in the large pot of your choosing on high heat.
  2. While searing, make a cornstarch-water slush with equal parts 2 tablespoons cornstarch and water.
  3. After searing, lower heat to medium-high. Add onions, mushrooms, and half the cornstarch slush. Continue cooking and stirring until onions are clear.
  4. Add entire pack of Golden curry, and 2 tablespoons of water. Stir regularly until the curry has broken apart. It likely wont dissolve completely.
  5. Add potatoes, carrots, water, soy sauce, worcestershire sauce, Maggi seasoning, mirin (or alternative) to pot and stir.
  6. Cook on medium until meat, potatoes, and carrots are all tender, and stew is thickened. Add corn starch as needed to thicken stew. (About 2-3 hours)
